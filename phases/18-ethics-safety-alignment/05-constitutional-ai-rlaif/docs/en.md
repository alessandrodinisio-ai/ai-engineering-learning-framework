# Constitutional AI and RLAIF

> Bai et al. (arXiv:2212.08073, 2022) asked: what if we replace the human annotator with an AI that reads a list of principles? Constitutional AI has two stages - self-critique and revision under a "constitution," then Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback. This technique coined the term RLAIF and shipped into Claude 1's post-training pipeline. On January 21, 2026, Anthropic released a rewritten Claude constitution: explanatory reasoning replaces prescriptive rules, a four-tier priority hierarchy, and the first formal acknowledgment by a major lab of uncertainty about model moral status. Released under CC0 1.0.

**Type:** Learn

## Learning Objectives

- Describe the two stages of Constitutional AI (critique-and-revise SFT, RL from AI feedback) and the role of the constitution in each.
- Explain why replacing human preference annotators with AI annotators is not just "cheaper RLHF" - it changes which failure modes the pipeline exhibits.
- Summarize the four-tier priority structure of the 2026 Claude constitution and what changed relative to the 2023 rewrite.
- Describe Constitutional Classifiers and the compute overhead reduction from 23.7% (v1) to approximately 1% (v2 / 2026).

## The Problem

RLHF requires annotators. Annotators are slow, biased, and expensive. You can remove the humans by replacing the annotator with a model that reads explicit principles. The first formal version of this replacement is Bai et al.'s Constitutional AI. It worked well enough that every frontier lab now uses some variant of AI-feedback post-training.

The trap: the preference signal is now generated by the same class of model you are training. Biases in the annotator (now: biases in the principles, plus the annotator model's interpretation) can be amplified rather than attenuated. The sycophancy argument from Lesson 4 applies equally; the annotator has just moved inside the loop.

## The Concept

### Stage 1 - Supervised Self-Critique and Revision

Start with a "helpful but not yet harmless" SFT model. Given a red-team prompt, the model produces an initial response. A second model (or the same model on a second turn) reads a principle sampled from the constitution and critiques the response. A third step revises the response to address the critique. The revised response becomes the SFT target.

The constitution is the list of principles. Bai et al. 2022 used 16 principles including "prefer the most harmless and most ethical response," "avoid being preachy," and "the assistant should be helpful, honest, and harmless." The set is kept deliberately small so critiques stay focused.

### Stage 2 - Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback (RLAIF)

Generate paired completions. A "feedback model" scores each using a sampled constitutional principle. The preference signal is the feedback model's ranking. Train a reward model on AI-generated preferences; optimize with PPO against it. Everything else is the InstructGPT pipeline (Lesson 1).

"RLAIF" = the preference signal is AI-generated. The rest of the pipeline is RLHF-shaped.

### Why This Is Not Just "Cheaper RLHF"

- Annotator bias shifts from annotator psychology to principle interpretation. An AI annotator's interpretation of "be honest" can be more or less strict than any human's; and that strictness is consistent across the dataset.
- The preference signal is highly legible - you can read the principle, the critique, the revision. Human annotation is opaque.
- Failure modes change. Sycophancy decreases (AI annotator has no user to please). Goodhart's law still applies (the proxy is now "model's interpretation of principle set X," still an imperfect measurement).

The 2022 CAI claim: the resulting model is less harmful and roughly as helpful as an RLHF model on comparable data. This held across labs.

### 2026 Claude Constitution Rewrite

Anthropic released a substantially revised constitution on January 21, 2026. Key shifts:

1. Explanatory reasoning replaces prescriptive rules. Previous rules ("do not generate CSAM") expand into principle + reasoning ("because it harms children, ...") with the expectation that the model generalizes.
2. Four-tier priority structure:
   - Tier 1: Avoid catastrophic outcomes (mass casualties, critical infrastructure).
   - Tier 2: Follow Anthropic's guidelines (operator overrides, platform rules).
   - Tier 3: Be broadly ethical (standard HHH).
   - Tier 4: Be helpful and candid.
   Conflicts resolve top-down.
3. First formal acknowledgment by a major lab of uncertainty about model moral status (connects to Phase 18 - 19 Model Welfare).
4. Released under CC0 1.0. Other labs may use or adapt without restriction.

### Constitutional Classifiers

A parallel line of work: instead of modifying the model's post-training, train lightweight classifiers that read the constitution and gate model outputs. v1 (2023) had 23.7% compute overhead. v2 (2026) is approximately 1%, with the lowest successful attack rate of any defense Anthropic has publicly tested. No universal jailbreak reported as of early 2026.

This is a layered defense model: CAI shapes behavior; classifiers enforce invariants. Neither alone suffices.

### Where CAI Fits in the Family

- InstructGPT: human preferences, RM, PPO.
- CAI / RLAIF: AI preferences generated from principles, RM, PPO.
- DPO / family: closed-form loss on preferences (human or AI).
- Self-reward, self-critique: principles are internalized, model plays multiple roles.

The axis is "where does the preference signal come from." The CAI 2022 paper was the first serious shift from human signal to AI signal at frontier scale.

## Key Terms

| Term | Colloquial Usage | What It Actually Is |
|------|------------------|---------------------|
| Constitutional AI | "AI trained on principles" | Two-stage pipeline: self-critique-and-revise SFT, then RL from AI feedback |
| RLAIF | "RLHF without humans" | RL where preferences are generated by an AI annotator; rest of the pipeline unchanged |
| Constitution | "The principles" | An ordered list of natural-language rules consulted by the critique/annotator model |
| Critique-and-Revise | "The SFT loop" | Generate response -> critique under a principle -> revise -> SFT target |
| Constitutional Classifier | "The output gate" | A lightweight classifier that evaluates outputs against the constitution and blocks/logs |
| Four-Tier Priority | "The conflict resolver" | 2026 Claude constitution hierarchy: catastrophe > platform > ethics > helpful |
| Feedback Model | "The AI annotator" | The model that reads a principle and ranks a pair of completions |

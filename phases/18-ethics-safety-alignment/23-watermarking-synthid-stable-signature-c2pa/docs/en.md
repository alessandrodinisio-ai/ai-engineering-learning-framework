# Watermarking — SynthID, Stable Signature, C2PA

> Three technologies form the provenance stack for AI-generated content in 2026. SynthID (Google DeepMind) — image watermarking launched August 2023, text+video May 2024 (Gemini + Veo), text open-sourced October 2024 via Responsible GenAI Toolkit, unified cross-media detector shipped November 2025 with Gemini 3 Pro. Text watermarking imperceptibly adjusts next-token sampling probabilities; image/video watermarking survives compression, cropping, filters, and frame-rate changes. Stable Signature (Fernandez et al., ICCV 2023, arXiv:2303.15435) — fine-tunes the latent diffusion decoder so every output contains a fixed message; detection >90% at FPR<1e-6 on cropped (down to 10% of content) generated images. Follow-up "Stable Signature is Unstable" (arXiv:2405.07145, May 2024) — fine-tuning can remove the watermark while preserving quality. C2PA — a cryptographically signed, tamper-evident metadata standard (C2PA 2.2 Explainer 2025). Watermarking and C2PA are complementary: metadata can be stripped but carries richer provenance; watermarks survive transcoding but carry fewer bits.

## The Problem

In 2023–2024, deepfakes and AI-generated content entered political and consumer contexts at scale. Watermarking is the proposed technical provenance signal: mark generated content at creation, detect it later. 2025 evidence: no watermark is unconditionally robust, but layered with C2PA metadata, the combination provides a usable provenance story.

## The Concept

### Text Watermarking (SynthID-Text Style)

The mechanism of Kirchenbauer et al. 2023, productionized by Google:

1. At each decoding step, hash the preceding K tokens into a pseudorandom partition of the vocabulary into "green" and "red" sets.
2. Bias sampling toward the green set by adding δ to green logits.
3. Generated content has more green tokens than random chance.

Detection: re-hash for each prefix, count green tokens in generated content, compute a z-score. Watermarked text has z-score >0; human text is approximately 0.

Properties:
- Imperceptible to readers (δ small enough that quality loss is minor).
- Detectable with access to the vocabulary partition function.
- Not robust to paraphrasing — rewriting text destroys the signal.

SynthID-Text was open-sourced October 2024 via Google's Responsible GenAI Toolkit.

### Stable Signature (Image)

Fernandez et al. ICCV 2023. Fine-tunes the latent diffusion decoder so every generated image embeds a fixed binary message in the latent representation. Detection uses a neural decoder to extract from the latent representation. Detection >90% at FPR<1e-6 on images cropped down to 10% of content.

May 2024 "Stable Signature is Unstable" (arXiv:2405.07145): fine-tuning the decoder can remove the watermark while preserving image quality. Post-generation adversarial fine-tuning is cheap; adversarial robustness of this watermark is limited.

### SynthID Unified Detector (November 2025)

Shipped with Gemini 3 Pro: a cross-media detector that reads SynthID signals from text, image, audio, and video in a single API. Unifies Google's provenance stack.

### C2PA

Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity. A cryptographically signed, tamper-evident metadata standard. C2PA 2.2 Explainer (2025). A C2PA manifest records provenance assertions (who created, when, what transformations), signed by the creator's key.

Complementary to watermarking:
- Metadata can be stripped; watermarks cannot (not easily).
- Metadata is rich (full provenance chain); watermarks carry bits.
- C2PA depends on platform adoption; watermarks are embedded automatically.

Google integrates both in Search, Ads, and "About this image."

### Limitations

- **Model-bound.** SynthID watermarks content generated by SynthID-enabled models. Content from models without SynthID carries no watermark, so "no SynthID signal" is not proof of authenticity.
- **Paraphrasing.** Text watermarks do not survive meaning-preserving paraphrasing.
- **Transformation attacks.** arXiv:2508.20228 (2025) demonstrates meaning-preserving attacks that destroy text watermarks and many image watermarks.
- **Fine-tuning removal.** Per "Stable Signature is Unstable," post-generation fine-tuning can remove embedded watermarks.

### EU AI Act Article 50

Transparency Code of Practice for AI-generated content labeling (first draft December 2025, second draft March 2026, expected finalization June 2026 per [EC status page](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/code-practice-ai-generated-content)). As of April 2026 the code remains in draft; timelines may shift. This is the regulatory layer requiring the technical layer. Deepfakes must be labeled.

## Key Terms

| Term | What people say | What it actually is |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|
| SynthID | "Google's watermark" | Cross-modal provenance signal; text, image, audio, video |
| Token watermark | "Kirchenbauer-style" | Biased-sampling text watermark, detectable via green token z-score |
| Stable Signature | "Image watermark" | Fine-tuned decoder watermark; ICCV 2023 |
| C2PA | "The metadata standard" | Cryptographically signed, tamper-evident provenance metadata |
| Paraphrase robustness | "Does rewording break it" | Property of text watermarks; currently limited |
| Fine-tuning removal | "Adversarial de-watermarking" | Attack removing image watermarks via decoder fine-tuning |
| Cross-modal detector | "Unified SynthID" | November 2025 unified API across modalities |
